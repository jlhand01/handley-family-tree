Biography for Robert L. Handley

Life Story of Robert L. Handley

Born February 10, 1946

Early Life

I was born on February 10, 1946, in Tillar, Arkansas, to David Handley Sr. and Verna Mae Rucker Handley. I am the eighth of sixteen children and the fifth of twelve boys.

Our family income was below the poverty line, and I was seriously educationally deprived. Because of working on the farm, I rarely attended school more than two days a week before Christmas, and the same happened in April and May. As a result, I struggled through elementary, middle, and high school.

In 1965, I graduated from Wolfe Project High School at the age of 19.

Education

In September 1965, I enrolled at AM&N College. Because I was financially deprived, the only way I could attend college was through the National Defense Student Loan and by participating in work study.

In 1969, I completed the requirements for graduation and earned a B.S. degree in Math Education.

I went on to pursue additional studies, earning:

A Master’s degree in Education Administration from the University of Arkansas, Fayetteville.

A Doctorate degree in D.Min and Pastoral Ministry from St. Martin College and Seminary, Milwaukee, Wisconsin.

Career in Education

After graduating in 1969, I was hired as the bookstore manager at AM&N College, a position I held until 1975.

I then became a math teacher at Southeast Middle School in the Pine Bluff School District. In addition to teaching, I also served as a bus driver for the district. Later, I advanced to become assistant principal and then principal at the same school.

Though I retired, I was later rehired as interim superintendent for the district, followed by serving as principal of Pine Bluff High School. I officially retired from the district in 2012.

Ministry

I accepted the call to ministry and in February 1977, I became pastor of Mt. Harmony Missionary Baptist Church. I have faithfully served as pastor of this church for over 37 years.

Through ministry, I have taught, preached, and evangelized in churches across America, as well as in parts of Europe and Africa.

Service and Leadership

I served 15 years on the Arkansas State Claims Commission.

In addition, I have held leadership roles including:

President of the District Congress

Moderator of the District Association

President of the Arkansas Congress of Christian Education

Family

I am married to Carrie L. Rayford, and together we have three children: Thaddeus, Leslie, and Cassandra.

We are also blessed with five grandchildren: Thaddeus Jr., Raven, Kimarra, Carrington Mills, and Barry Joe Minor.

Faith and Legacy

From education to ministry, my life has been centered on service, leadership, and faith. To God be the Glory.
