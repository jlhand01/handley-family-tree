Life Story of Joshua Handley

Early Life

I was the tenth child out of sixteen children and was born at home in Tillar, Arkansas, with Sweet Ola James serving as the midwife.

I was given the nickname “Juice” because I loved to drink sorghum. There were times when I stayed home to work the fields, because there was no one else to do it and Daddy made us.

Growing up, I did not have a love for school until the seventh grade. Holidays were always plentiful in food, though not many gifts.

Moving to Pine Bluff

On February 17, 1967, our family moved to Pine Bluff in a big old red truck. We lived in a three-bedroom home, with two to three children sharing a bed. Despite those conditions, we were grateful for what we had—and in fact, we were very happy.

Education

I graduated number two in my class of more than 100 students at Coleman High School.

I attended AM&N College (which became the University of Arkansas at Pine Bluff in 1972) from 1970 to 1974, earning a B.A. degree in Mathematics.

Later, I pursued graduate studies and obtained a Master’s degree in Education from the University of Arkansas at Fayetteville.

Career in Education

I began my teaching career in the fall of 1974, teaching 7th through 12th grade mathematics in Altheimer, Arkansas.

After my first year of teaching, I underwent open brain surgery in the summer of 1975.

I continued to serve in education and retired from teaching in the Pine Bluff School District in 2010.

Later Years

Now retired, I am enjoying life and giving thanks to God for each day He blesses me with.
